The Pivot to Technology

My professional journey is rooted in a deep desire to solve complex, real-world problems. I began my career on the frontlines of healthcare, graduating with a Diploma as a Kenya Registered Community Health Nurse (KRCHN). While nursing taught me profound empathy, rapid clinical triage, and how to manage high-stakes environments, I increasingly noticed the critical gaps in the digital systems supporting patient care. My innate curiosity continuously gravitated towards technology—specifically, how robust software architecture and data-driven systems could scale solutions to reach far beyond a single hospital ward.

Driven by this realization, I made a decisive pivot into the tech industry. I formalized my technical skills by earning a Diploma in Information & Communication Technology with Distinction from Siaya National Polytechnic, building a rigorous baseline in network infrastructure, systems diagnostics, and software development. Today, I am aggressively expanding my expertise as a Computer Science undergraduate at the University of the People (USA). Securing a scholarship for this degree has been a pivotal milestone. It empowers me to merge my analytical, life-saving healthcare foundation with advanced software engineering principles, allowing me to architect digital experiences that truly impact users.
